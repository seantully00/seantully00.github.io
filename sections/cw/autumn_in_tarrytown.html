Autumn in Tarrytown

I heard a gong like an abrupt alarm,
an awakening, and there I was,
zipping across Main Street, aware
of the too cold air, of the death of leaves,
and of the charm on her ankle.
She was crossing ahead
of me, and that cheap copper chain
jangled like a teacup on a saucer,
like a reindeer's bell, like glass shattering,
like anything loud and everything fragile.
The firehouse's horn went off and she jumped,
and I jumped, and we both hopped the curb
to the sidewalk. I stopped and felt the hairs
on my arms (they tingled at their roots),
and watched a fire truck
barge and shriek its way towards
a certain amount of danger.
